4.0 out of 5 stars20-25 degree drop
Byjust someone who buys stuff from time to timeon August 11, 2013
Verified Purchase
This seemed to work as advertised. On arrival, I plugged it into the AC, then placed a meat thermometer into an empty glass, probe end up, to measure the interior air temp - it was about 20-25 degrees less than ambient (about 75). I then placed a single room-temp unopened soda can with a meat thermometer duct-taped to the side. A few hours later, it was cooled about 20-25 degrees below ambient temp. The fan was moderate in noise - noticeable, but not too bad in a large room. The shelf is removable. It came with both AC and 12 volt (car lighter) cords.

Peltier-effect devices take time to move a large quantity of heat from the interior to the exterior (e.g., when cooling a large thermal mass from room temp). I suspect it may work better for smaller items, or for pre-cooled items. In my house, this cooler did not reach the suggested 40 degree refrigerator temperature, so I doubt it would replace a compressor type of refrigerator in a warm climate. However, it did make an unopened can of soda noticeably cool.
